# Interpretation limitations

- OCS records submissions received through contributing online systems, not all
  requests to a practice.
- GPAD records scheduled or planned appointment-system activity, not complete
  workload, capacity or unmet demand.
- CBT coverage is supplier- and participation-dependent and includes mapping
  limitations for accounts serving multiple practices.
- A submission, appointment and call cannot be linked to the same patient.
- Separate source totals must not be added and labelled total demand.
- Temporal correspondence does not establish channel substitution or causality.
- Rates per 1,000 registered patient-months normalise recorded activity by list
  exposure; they are not direct measures of access quality.
- Matched cohorts are selected populations and can differ systematically from
  practices excluded by source availability.
- Annual profiles exclude practices without complete twelve-month evidence.
- The April 2025 Y60 CBT day/time gap remains missing and explicitly flagged in
  the reference temporal output.

## Deliberate analytical safeguards

- No source absence is converted to zero activity.
- No OCS, GPAD and CBT counts are combined into a single activity total.
- No composite access score or practice ranking is created by the integration.
- No wider-area value is presented as though it were a practice-level value.
- No comparison presents different observation periods as though they were
  aligned.
- No matched-source result is generalised to practices excluded by its
  reporting requirements without an explicit population caveat.
